BNS Section - 1

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 1

Short Title, Extent, and Commencement

(1) This Act may be called the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023.

(2) It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint, and different dates may be appointed for different provisions of the Sanhita.

(3) Every person shall be liable to punishment under this Sanhita and not otherwise for every act or omission contrary to the provisions thereof, of which he shall be guilty within India.

(4) Any person liable, by any law for the time being in force in India, to be tried for an offence committed beyond India shall be dealt with according to the provisions of this Sanhita for any act committed beyond India in the same manner as if such act had been committed within India.

(5) The provisions of this Sanhita apply also to any offence committed by

(a) any citizen of India in any place without and beyond India;
(b) any person on any ship or aircraft registered in India wherever it may be;
(c) any person in any place without and beyond India committing offence targeting a computer resource located in India.

Explanation
In this section the word “offence” includes every act committed outside India which, if committed in India, would be punishable under this Sanhita.

Illustrations

A, who is a citizen of India, commits a murder in any place without and beyond India, he can be tried and convicted of murder in any place in India in which he may be found.

(6) Nothing in this Sanhita shall affect the provisions of any Act for punishing mutiny and desertion of officers, soldiers, sailors or airmen in the service of the Government of India or the provisions of any special or local law.


Key Points in BNS-01

  1. Short Title and Commencement:  

    • The Act is formally titled the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023.  

    • It will come into effect on a date notified by the Central Government. Different provisions may come into force at different times.  

  2. Applicability of Punishment:  

    • Every individual in India is subject to punishment under this Act for acts or omissions that violate its provisions. This includes any offenses committed within India’s borders.  

  3. Extra-Territorial Jurisdiction:  

    • Individuals can be punished under this Act for crimes committed beyond India, as long as they are liable under Indian law. Offenses committed outside India are treated as if they occurred within India.  

  4. Offenses Committed Abroad:  

    • The Act extends its jurisdiction to:  

      • Indian citizens committing offenses abroad.  

      • Crimes committed on Indian-registered ships or aircraft, regardless of their location.  

      • Crimes targeting computer resources located in India, even if committed by individuals outside India.  

This provision reflects India's approach to holding its citizens accountable for actions beyond its borders, ensuring that crimes affecting Indian interests are punishable, regardless of where they occur.
