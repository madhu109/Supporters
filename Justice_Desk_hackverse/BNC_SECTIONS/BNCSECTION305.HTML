BNS Section - 305

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 305

Theft in a dwelling house, or means of transportation or place of worship, etc

Whoever commits theft

(a) in any building, tent or vessel used as a human dwelling or used for the custody of property; or (b) of any means of transport used f
or the transport of goods or passengers; or
(c) of any article or goods from any means of transport used for the transport of goods or passengers; or
(d) of idol or icon in any place of worship; or
(e) of any property of the Government or of a local authority,
shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years and shall also be liable to fine.

Key Points in BNS-305

Key Points in BNS-305

1.Scope of Theft:
 

  • BNS-305 outlines specific categories of theft that involve particular types of locations, objects, or property. The statute covers theft committed under several distinct circumstances:
     

(a) Theft in a Building, Tent, or Vessel:
 

  • Definition: Theft occurring within any building, tent, or vessel used as a human dwelling or for the custody of property.
     

  • Implication: This includes homes, shelters, and any enclosed or covered areas used to store or safeguard belongings.
     

(b) Theft of Means of Transport:
 

  • Definition: Theft involving any means of transport utilized for carrying goods or passengers.
     

  • Implication: This includes vehicles like cars, trucks, trains, ships, and airplanes used for transporting goods or people.
     

(c) Theft from Means of Transport:
 

  • Definition: Theft of articles or goods specifically from any means of transport mentioned in (b).
     

  • Implication: This pertains to theft directly from vehicles or vessels used for transportation, including cargo theft.
     

(d) Theft of Idols or Icons:
 

  • Definition: Theft of religious idols or icons from places of worship.
     

  • Implication: This involves theft from temples, churches, mosques, or any other religious sites where sacred objects are kept.
     

(e) Theft of Government or Local Authority Property:
 

  • Definition: Theft involving any property belonging to the Government or a local authority.
     

  • Implication: This includes public property managed by government agencies or local bodies, such as offices, public facilities, and infrastructure.
     

2.Punishment for Theft:
 

  • Imprisonment: Offenders are punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years.
     

  • Fine: In addition to imprisonment, the offender is also liable to a fine.
     

Important Aspects:
 

  • Severity of Theft: Theft under these specific circumstances is considered more serious due to the nature of the property or location involved. The punishment reflects the gravity of stealing from areas or items that are particularly sensitive or significant.
     

  • Types of Property: The statute includes a range of property typesâ€”from personal belongings in dwellings to public property and religious artifacts. This broad coverage ensures protection of diverse categories of property from theft.
     

  • Punishment Severity: The imprisonment term of up to seven years indicates that theft in these contexts is treated as a serious offense, acknowledging the potential impact and harm caused by such crimes.


Summary:

BNS-305 addresses theft committed under specific circumstances, including theft in places of human dwelling, means of transport, religious sites, and public property. The law prescribes severe penalties, including up to seven years of imprisonment and fines, recognizing the heightened seriousness of theft in these contexts.
