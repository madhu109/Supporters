BNS Section - 16

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 16

Act done pursuant to the judgment or order of Court

Nothing which is done in pursuance of, or which is warranted by the judgment or order of, a Court; if done whilst such judgment or order remains in force, is an offence, notwithstanding the Court may have had no jurisdiction to pass such judgment or order, provided the person doing the act in good faith believes that the Court had such jurisdiction.

Key Points in BNS-16

Key Points for BNS-16:
 

  1. Protection for Actions Taken Under Judicial Orders:

    BNS-16 provides legal protection for individuals who carry out actions in compliance with a court's judgment or order. If someone follows the directive of a court order, they are not considered to have committed a crime, even if the order is later found to be flawed or the court lacked proper authority. The central idea is that no liability will arise for executing such orders.
     

  2. Jurisdiction of the Court:

    The provision specifically accounts for situations where the court issuing the order may not have had jurisdiction over the matter. Jurisdiction refers to the court's legal authority to hear a case and pass orders on it. However, if the person carrying out the court's decision is unaware of the court’s lack of jurisdiction and acts in accordance with the order, they will not be held criminally liable.
     

  3. Good Faith Requirement:

    For this protection to apply, the person acting under the court’s directive must have done so in good faith, meaning they genuinely believed that the court had the proper jurisdiction or authority to issue the order. Acting in good faith means the person had no intent to violate the law or circumvent legal processes.
     

  4. Valid Court Orders in Force:

    The law covers actions taken as long as the court’s order or judgment is still in force. This means that if the judgment has not been overturned or invalidated by a higher court or legal authority, actions taken under it will be protected under BNS-16.
     

  5. Non-liability for Flawed Orders:

    Even if the court eventually turns out to have no legal right to pass the order, or if the order itself is defective, the individual following the court’s instructions will not be punished as long as they believed the court had jurisdiction. This principle is important in preventing individuals from being penalized for simply following the court’s instructions.
     

  6. Legal Integrity and Public Trust:

    This provision helps to ensure that public officials and individuals can act upon court orders without fear of later criminal prosecution if the order was mistaken or flawed. It maintains the integrity of legal processes and ensures that the public can trust judicial decisions without hesitation.


In essence, BNS-16 upholds that no individual should be penalized for acting on the basis of a court's judgment or order, provided they acted in good faith and while the order was still in effect, even if the court's jurisdiction was later questioned.
