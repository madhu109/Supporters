BNS Section - 23

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 23

Act of a person incapable of judgment by reason of intoxication caused against his will

Nothing is an offence which is done by a person who, at the time of doing it, is, by reason of intoxication, incapable of knowing the nature of the act, or that he is doing what is either wrong, or contrary to law; unless that the thing which intoxicated him was administered to him without his knowledge or against his will.

Key Points in BNS-23

Key Points for BNS-23:
 

  1. Legal Immunity Due to Involuntary Intoxication:

    BNS-23 provides immunity from criminal responsibility for individuals who, due to involuntary intoxication, are incapable of understanding the nature or wrongness of their actions at the time of committing an act. If intoxication prevents someone from comprehending what they are doing or realizing that it is illegal or wrong, they may not be held liable under this provision.
     

  2. Involuntary Intoxication:

    The law specifically applies when the intoxication is caused against the person's will or without their knowledge. For example, if someone is drugged or unknowingly given alcohol or other intoxicants, and as a result commits an act that would otherwise be criminal, BNS-23 protects them from being punished for that act. The intoxication must be involuntaryâ€”the person must not have willingly consumed the intoxicating substance.
     

  3. Incapacity to Judge the Act:

    The critical factor is whether, due to the intoxication, the person is incapable of understanding:
     

    • The nature of the act they are committing (i.e., they are unaware of what they are doing).
       

    • Whether the act is wrong or contrary to the law.
      For example, if someone is forced into a state of intoxication and then commits an offense without realizing the implications of their actions, this provision could absolve them of criminal responsibility.
       

  4. Voluntary Intoxication Not a Defense:

    It is important to note that voluntary intoxication is not a defense under BNS-23. If a person consumes alcohol or drugs willingly and then commits an offense, they can still be held criminally responsible for their actions. The provision only applies when the intoxication is not self-induced.
     

  5. State of Mind at the Time of the Offense:

    BNS-23 emphasizes the person's mental state at the time of the offense. If, due to involuntary intoxication, the person is in such a state that they are unable to understand what they are doing or that their actions are wrong, they are shielded from liability. This reflects a legal principle that criminal responsibility is based on the ability to form intent and understand one's actions.
     

  6. Administration of Intoxicants:

    For the immunity under BNS-23 to apply, the intoxicant must have been administered to the person without their knowledge or against their will. This could occur through deliberate actions by another party, such as spiking a drink or coercing someone to consume a substance without consent.


Conclusion:

BNS-23 provides legal protection to individuals who, due to involuntary intoxication, are incapable of judging their actions or understanding that they are committing an offense. The provision is designed to prevent unjust punishment in cases where the individualâ€™s mental state is compromised due to intoxication caused against their will or without their knowledge. However, voluntary intoxication does not offer any defense under this law, and offenders can still be held accountable if they willingly consume intoxicating substances.
