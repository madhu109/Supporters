BNS Section - 44

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 44

Right of private defence against deadly assault when there is risk of harm to innocent person

If in the exercise of the right of private defence against an assault which reasonably causes the apprehension of death, the defender be so situated that he cannot effectually exercise that right without risk of harm to an innocent person, his right of private defence extends to the running of that risk.

Illustrations

A is attacked by a mob who attempt to murder him. He cannot effectually exercise his right of private defence without firing on the mob, and he cannot fire without risk of harming young children who are mingled with the mob. A commits no offence if by so firing he harms any of the children.

Example:
Vijay is attacked by a mob and he can only protect himself by risking harm to the innocent bystanders. Here, Vijay is allowed to defend himself even at the expense of innocent people. 

Key Points in BNS-44

Key Points of BNS-44

BNS-44 addresses situations where exercising the right of private defence against an assault that reasonably causes apprehension of death might unintentionally harm an innocent person. This provision clarifies that the right of private defence can extend to such risks under specific circumstances.

Important Points of BNS-44:
 

  1. Extension of Defence Right:
     

    • When defending oneself or others from an assault that causes a reasonable fear of death, and if the defender is in a position where exercising this right might inadvertently harm an innocent person, the right of private defence still applies. This means that the defender can take necessary actions even if there's a risk of harming someone who is not involved in the assault.
       

  2. Balancing Rights and Risks:
     

    • The provision acknowledges that in certain critical situations, the necessity of defending against a severe threat (like an imminent death) might involve unavoidable risks to others. The defender is thus legally permitted to act in such a way that could potentially endanger innocent individuals if it's the only way to effectively exercise the right of defence.
       

  3. Reasonable Apprehension:
     

    • The assault must create a reasonable apprehension of death. This standard means that the fear of death must be justified and based on the actual threat posed by the assailant.
       

Definitions:
 

  • Right of Private Defence: The legal entitlement to protect oneself or others from harm using reasonable force.
     

  • Reasonable Apprehension of Death: A justified belief that the assault could result in death if defensive action is not taken.
     

  • Innocent Person: An individual who is not involved in the assault or threat and is not responsible for the danger.
     

Example Scenario:

If a person is being attacked in a way that makes them fear for their life, and defending themselves or others would likely involve a risk to a bystander (such as using a weapon in a crowded area), their right to defend themselves extends to the risk posed to that innocent bystander. This means that while the defender must be cautious and act reasonably, they are not required to forgo their defence solely to avoid risk to an uninvolved third party.

Conclusion:

BNS-44 ensures that in critical situations where the right to private defence is necessary to prevent death, the defender's actions are legally protected even if there is a risk of causing harm to an innocent person. This provision highlights the balance between the need for self-defence and the potential consequences of such actions on those not involved in the threat.
