BNS Section - 35

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 35

Right of private defence of the body and of property

Every person has a right, subject to the restrictions contained in section 37, to defend
(a) his own body, and the body of any other person, against any offence affecting the human body;
(b) the property, whether movable or immovable, of himself or of any other person, against any act which is an offence falling under the definition of theft, robbery, mischief or criminal trespass, or which is an attempt to commit theft, robbery, mischief or criminal trespass.

Example:
Vijay sees someone attacking Priya and therefore pushes and hits the attacker. Here, Vijay has exercised the right to private defence by helping another person out of danger, and is therefore protected under the law. 

Key Points in BNS-35

Key Points of BNS-35

BNS-35 outlines the right of an individual to defend both themselves and others, as well as property, against certain offences. This right is a fundamental principle of self-preservation, but it is subject to the restrictions laid down in BNS-37.

1. Defence of One’s Own Body and Others:
 

  • Every person has the right to defend their own body or the body of another person.
     

  • This right applies when the defence is against an offence affecting the human body, such as physical assaults or harm.
     

2. Defence of Property (Movable or Immovable):
 

  • The right also extends to defending both movable (e.g., personal belongings) and immovable property (e.g., land or a house) from criminal acts.
     

  • The criminal acts that warrant defence include:
     

    • Theft: Unlawful taking of property.
       

    • Robbery: Theft accompanied by violence or intimidation.
       

    • Mischief: Intentionally causing damage to property.
       

    • Criminal Trespass: Illegally entering someone’s property.
       

3. Defending Against Attempts:
 

  • The right of defence also applies when someone attempts to commit any of the mentioned offences, even if the crime is not completed.
     

Important Definitions:
 

  • Offence Affecting the Human Body: This includes any action that causes physical harm or threats of harm to a person.
     

  • Theft and Robbery: Acts that involve unlawfully taking someone’s property, with robbery involving an element of force or fear.
     

  • Mischief: Causing wrongful loss or damage to someone’s property intentionally.
     

  • Criminal Trespass: Entering someone’s property without permission with the intent to commit an offence or intimidate.
     

Conclusion:

BNS-35 gives individuals the right to defend themselves, others, and property from certain crimes. However, this right is not unlimited and is subject to the conditions stated in BNS-37. The defence must be proportional and in good faith.
