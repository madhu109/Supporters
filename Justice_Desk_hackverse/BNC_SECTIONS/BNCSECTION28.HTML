BNS Section - 28

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 28

Consent known to be given under fear or misconception

A consent is not such a consent as is intended by any section of this Sanhita,
(a) if the consent is given by a person under fear of injury, or under a misconception of fact, and if the person doing the act knows, or has reason to believe, that the consent was given in consequence of such fear or misconception; or
(b) if the consent is given by a person who, from mental illness, or intoxication, is unable to understand the nature and consequence of that to which he gives his consent; or
(c) unless the contrary appears from the context, if the consent is given by a person who is under twelve years of age.

Example
Vijay is threatened by Rakesh at gunpoint, to sign a property document and consent to transfer his property to Rakesh. Here, Vijay's consent is invalid since it was given under fear of injury.

Key Points in BNS-28

Key Points on BNS-28

BNS-28 defines situations where consent is not valid under the law. This provision ensures that consent must be free, informed, and voluntary to be considered legally acceptable.

1. Invalid Consent Due to Fear or Misconception:
 

  • Clause (a): Consent given under fear of injury or misconception of fact is not valid. If the person performing the act knows or has reason to believe that the consent was obtained due to fear or misunderstanding, the consent is deemed invalid.
     

    • Example: If a person agrees to an action due to a threat or false information, the consent is not genuine.
       

2. Inability to Understand Due to Mental Illness or Intoxication:
 

  • Clause (b): Consent given by someone who is unable to understand the nature and consequences of their decision due to mental illness or intoxication is not considered valid. This ensures protection for individuals who may not be in full control of their reasoning.
     

    • Example: A person who is intoxicated or mentally incapacitated cannot provide valid consent.
       

3. Consent by Minors:
 

  • Clause (c): Consent provided by a person who is under 12 years of age is generally invalid, unless a specific law states otherwise. This provision protects minors who may not fully comprehend the implications of their actions.
     

    • Example: A child under 12 cannot legally consent to actions that have significant consequences, such as medical procedures or contractual agreements.
       

Purpose of BNS-28:

This section ensures that consent is valid only when it is given freely, without coercion, and by individuals who are capable of understanding the implications of their consent. It protects vulnerable individuals, such as minors or those with mental health issues, from exploitation.
