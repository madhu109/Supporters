BNS Section - 32

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 32

Act to which a person compelled by threats

Except murder, and offences against the State punishable with death, nothing is an offence which is done by a person who is compelled to do it by threats, which, at the time of doing it, reasonably cause the apprehension that instant death to that person will otherwise be the consequence:
Provided the person doing the act did not of his own accord, or from a reasonable apprehension of harm to himself short of instant death, place himself in the situation by which he became subject to such constraint.

Explanation 1
A person who, of his own accord, or by reason of a threat of being beaten, joins a gang of dacoits, knowing their character, is not entitled to the benefit of this exception, on the ground of his having been compelled by his associates to do anything that is an offence by law.

Explanation 2
A person seized by a gang of dacoits, and forced, by threat of instant death, to do a thing which is an offence by law; for example, a smith compelled to take his tools and to force the door of a house for the dacoits to enter and plunder it, is entitled to the benefit of this exception.

Example:
Vijay, a carpenter is forced to use his tools to break open a house, under threat of being killed by dacoits. Here, Vijay has committed no offence since he was forced to do an illegal activity under threat of instant death. 

Key Points in BNS-32

Key Points on BNS-32

BNS-32 provides an exception to criminal liability under certain conditions of duress or coercion. Here's a breakdown of the key points and definitions:

1. General Principle:
 

  • The principle is that nothing is considered an offence, except for murder and offences against the State punishable by death, if the person commits the act because they were compelled to do so by threats of immediate death.
     

  • This exception acknowledges situations where an individual is coerced into committing a crime under extreme pressure, such as a direct threat to their life.
     

2. Conditions for the Exception:
 

  • Compulsion by Threats: The act must be committed because of a threat that causes a reasonable apprehension of instant death. This means the threat must be immediate and credible enough to justify the individual's actions.
     

  • Not Self-Placed in Situation: The individual should not have voluntarily put themselves in a situation where they were subject to such threats. If they had reasonable apprehension of harm, short of instant death, and placed themselves in a risky situation knowingly, they cannot claim this exception.
     

3. Illustrative Example:
 

  • If someone is coerced into committing a crime (e.g., robbery) because of a threat that they will be killed immediately if they do not comply, and they had no prior intention or reasonable apprehension that would justify being in such a position, they are not considered criminally liable for the act.
     

  • However, if the person had put themselves in a situation where such threats were foreseeable, and they had reasonable apprehension of harm but not instant death, this exception does not apply.
     

4. Exclusions:
 

  • Murder: The exception does not apply to acts of murder, regardless of the threat faced.
     

  • Offences Against the State Punishable by Death: It also excludes offences that are against the State and punishable by death.
     

Purpose of BNS-32:

This section aims to provide a legal safeguard for individuals who are forced into committing criminal acts under extreme duress, ensuring they are not unjustly penalized when their actions are driven by a genuine fear of imminent death. It underscores the principle that criminal liability is not applicable when a person is coerced under such dire circumstances, as long as they did not willingly or foreseeably place themselves in that situation.
