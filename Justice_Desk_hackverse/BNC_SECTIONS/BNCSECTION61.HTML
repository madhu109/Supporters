BNS Section -61

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 61

Criminal conspiracy

(1)When two or more persons agree to do, or cause to be done
(a) an illegal act; or
(b) an act which is not illegal by illegal means, such an agreement is designated a criminal conspiracy:
Provided that no agreement except an agreement to commit an offence shall amount to a criminal conspiracy unless some act besides the agreement is done by one or more parties to such agreement in pursuance thereof.

Explanation
It is immaterial whether the illegal act is the ultimate object of such agreement, or is merely incidental to that object.
(2) Whoever is a party to a criminal conspiracy,
(a) to commit an offence punishable with death, imprisonment for life or rigorous imprisonment for a term of two years or upwards, shall, where no express provision is made in this Sanhita for the punishment of such a conspiracy, be punished in the same manner as if he had abetted such offence;
(b) other than a criminal conspiracy to commit an offence punishable as aforesaid shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term not exceeding six months, or with fine or with both.

Key Points in BNS-61

Key Points on BNS-61

BNS-61 addresses the legal concept of criminal conspiracy and the punishment for being part of such an agreement. It covers agreements to commit illegal acts or legal acts through illegal means.

1. Criminal Conspiracy Definition
 

  • A criminal conspiracy occurs when two or more persons agree to:
     

    • (a) Commit an illegal act.
       

    • (b) Do a legal act by illegal means.
       

  • This agreement is designated as a criminal conspiracy.


2. Criminal Conspiracy Requirements
 

  • Agreement: The agreement to commit an offence itself is sufficient for it to be considered a criminal conspiracy. No further action is required.
     

  • Additional Acts: For conspiracies not involving an offence, there must be some act done in pursuance of the agreement by one or more parties. The agreement alone is not enough.
     

3. Explanation
 

  • Incidental Illegal Acts: It is irrelevant whether the illegal act is the ultimate goal of the agreement or merely incidental to another objective. This means that even if the illegal act is a secondary part of a larger plan, it still constitutes a criminal conspiracy.


4. Punishment for Criminal Conspiracy

BNS-61 outlines different penalties depending on the nature of the conspiracy:

(a) Conspiracy to Commit a Serious Offence
 

  • Offences Punishable with Death, Life Imprisonment, or Rigorous Imprisonment for Two Years or More:
     

    • The conspirator shall be punished as if they had abetted the offence.
       

    • If no specific provision is made for the punishment of such a conspiracy, the conspirator receives the same punishment as for abetment of that offence.
       

(b) Conspiracy to Commit Less Serious Offences
 

  • For any other conspiracy not involving a serious offence as described above, the punishment shall be:
     

    • Imprisonment for a term up to six months.
       

    • Fine or both imprisonment and fine may be imposed.
       

5. Key Definitions and Clarifications
 

  • Illegal Act: Any act that is forbidden by law or is criminal in nature.
     

  • Legal Act by Illegal Means: An otherwise legal action performed using methods that are unlawful or criminal.
     

  • Agreement: The essential requirement for a criminal conspiracy. The parties must have come to a mutual understanding to commit the act or acts, either illegal or through illegal means.
     

6. Implications
 

  • Punishment for Agreement: The primary factor is the agreement between the parties, even if no illegal act is carried out.
     

  • Seriousness of the Offence: The punishment is based on the severity of the offence being conspired, with harsher penalties for more serious offences like murder or life imprisonment.
     

Summary

BNS-61 establishes that when two or more people agree to commit an illegal act or a legal act by illegal means, they form a criminal conspiracy. The provision allows for different penalties depending on the seriousness of the conspiracy. It is significant that for a conspiracy to commit a less serious offence, further acts must be done in pursuit of the conspiracy for it to be punishable. The explanation clarifies that it doesn't matter whether the illegal act is incidental to the main goal or the main objective itself, making it a broad-reaching law.


These definitions establish the foundational meanings of various terms used throughout the Sanhita, providing clarity for legal interpretation.
