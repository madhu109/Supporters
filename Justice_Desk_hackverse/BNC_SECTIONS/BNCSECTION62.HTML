BNS Section -62

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 62s

Punishment for attempting to commit offences punishable with imprisonment for life or other imprisonment

Whoever attempts to commit an offence punishable by this Sanhita with imprisonment for life or imprisonment, or to cause such an offence to be committed, and in such attempt does any act towards the commission of the offence, shall, where no express provision is made by this Sanhita for the punishment of such attempt, be punished with imprisonment of any description provided for the offence, for a term which may extend to one-half of the imprisonment for life or, as the case may be, one-half of the longest term of imprisonment provided for that offence, or with such fine as is provided for the offence, or with both.

Illustrations

(a) A makes an attempt to steal some jewels by breaking open a box, and finds after so opening the box, that there is no jewel in it. He has done an act towards the commission of theft, and therefore is guilty under this section.
(b) A makes an attempt to pick the pocket of Z by thrusting his hand into Z’s pocket. A fails in the attempt in consequence of Z’s having nothing in his pocket. A is guilty under this section.


Example:
Vijay goes to Rakesh’s house with the intention of stealing jewellery and tries to steal it by breaking the lock of his cupboard, but he is unable to steal anything because there is no jewellery in the trunk. In such a case, Vijay has acted towards the offence of stealing and he will therefore be guilty under this section.

Key Points in BNS-62

Key Points on BNS-62

BNS-62 deals with attempts to commit offences and outlines the punishment for such attempts when no specific provision is made for them in the Sanhita. Here's a breakdown of the important points and definitions:

1. Attempt to Commit an Offence
 

  • This provision applies when someone attempts to commit an offence punishable by imprisonment for life or any other term of imprisonment.
     

  • The act must involve a direct action toward the commission of the offence. This means the individual must have gone beyond preparation and taken concrete steps to commit the crime.
     

2. Applicable Offences
 

  • The section specifically targets attempts to commit offences that are punishable with imprisonment for life or imprisonment of any duration.
     

  • It covers both:
     

  • Attempting to commit the offence personally.
     

  • Causing or trying to cause the offence to be committed by another person.
     

3. Punishment for the Attempt
 

  • Where no express provision is made for punishing the attempt, the person attempting the offence shall be punished with:
     

  • Imprisonment: Up to one-half of the life imprisonment term or one-half of the longest term of imprisonment prescribed for that offence.
     

  • Fine: The person may also be punished with the same fine provided for the offence itself.
     

  • Both: The court may impose both imprisonment and a fine.
     

4. Important Definitions
 

  • Attempt: In legal terms, an attempt means that the individual has gone beyond mere intention or planning and taken concrete steps towards committing the offence.
     

  • Imprisonment for Life: A sentence where the convict is imprisoned for the rest of their natural life, unless released on parole or some form of clemency.
     

  • Imprisonment: The general term used for any sentence where the person is incarcerated for a specified period.
     

5. Scope of the Section
 

  • The section applies only in the absence of specific punishment provisions for attempts within the Sanhita. If a specific law prescribes the punishment for an attempt, that will take precedence.
     

  • This means BNS-62 serves as a general fallback provision for punishing attempts when the primary law regarding the offence does not explicitly mention an attempt.
     

6. Proportionality of Punishment
 

  • The punishment for an attempt is proportional to the seriousness of the crime attempted.
     

  • The maximum punishment is half of the full sentence that would have been given if the crime had been fully committed.
     

Summary

BNS-62 focuses on punishing individuals who attempt to commit offences punishable by imprisonment, including life imprisonment. If no specific punishment for the attempt is provided in the Sanhita, the person can be punished with up to half the term of the imprisonment prescribed for the full offence, a fine, or both. The provision ensures that even failed attempts to commit serious crimes are punishable by law.


These definitions establish the foundational meanings of various terms used throughout the Sanhita, providing clarity for legal interpretation.
