Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023

Home BNC Sections BNC SECTION 38

When the right of private defence of the body extends to causing death

The right of private defence of the body extends, under the restrictions specified in section 37, to the voluntary causing of death or of any other harm to the assailant, if the offence which occasions the exercise of the right be of any of the descriptions hereinafter enumerated, namely:

(a) such an assault as may reasonably cause the apprehension that death will otherwise be the consequence of such assault;
(b) such an assault as may reasonably cause the apprehension that grievous hurt will otherwise be the consequence of such assault;
(c) an assault with the intention of committing rape;
(d) an assault with the intention of gratifying unnatural lust;
(e) an assault with the intention of kidnapping or abducting;
(f) an assault with the intention of wrongfully confining a person, under circumstances which may reasonably cause him to apprehend that he will be unable to have recourse to the public authorities for his release;
(g) an act of throwing or administering acid or an attempt to throw or administer acid which may reasonably cause the apprehension that grievous hurt will otherwise be the consequence of such act.

Example:
A kidnapper assaults Anita with the intention to abduct her. She inturn kills him by use of a deadly weapon. Here, Anita has acted in private defence and is proctected under law. 

Key Points in BNS-38

Key Points of BNS-38

BNS-38 explains the situations under which a person has the right to voluntarily cause death or harm to an assailant while exercising their right of private defence, subject to the restrictions in BNS-37. This section provides legal justification for the use of lethal or harmful force in self-defence under certain severe circumstances.

Situations Where Private Defence Extends to Causing Death or Harm:
 

  1. Apprehension of Death:
     

    • Clause (a): If the assault may reasonably lead to the fear of death, a person has the right to use deadly force in defence.
       

  2. Apprehension of Grievous Hurt:
     

    • Clause (b): When the assault causes a reasonable fear of grievous injury (severe bodily harm), the defender can also use force, even if it leads to death.
       

  3. Assault with the Intent to Commit Rape:
     

    • Clause (c): The right of private defence extends to assaults aimed at committing rape. Lethal or harmful force can be used to protect oneself from such assaults.
       

  4. Assault with the Intent to Gratify Unnatural Lust:
     

    • Clause (d): In the case of an assault meant to gratify unnatural lust, the person being assaulted has the right to defend themselves with necessary force, even if it results in the death of the assailant.
       

  5. Kidnapping or Abduction:
     

    • Clause (e): If the assault involves an attempt to kidnap or abduct, the defender has the right to cause harm, including death, to prevent such an offence.
       

  6. Wrongful Confinement:
     

    • Clause (f): When someone is being wrongfully confined in a manner that prevents them from reaching public authorities, they have the right to use force, even to the extent of causing death, to escape the confinement.
       

  7. Acid Attacks:
     

    • Clause (g): The right of private defence also applies to acid attacks or attempted acid attacks. A person may defend themselves with lethal or harmful force if the attack reasonably causes apprehension of grievous hurt.
       

Important Definitions:
 

  • Apprehension of Death or Grievous Hurt: The reasonable belief or fear that an attack will lead to death or severe physical injury.
     

  • Unnatural Lust: Acts of sexual aggression that do not align with natural sexual practices, involving force or coercion.
     

  • Wrongful Confinement: Illegally holding someone against their will, making it impossible for them to access legal help.
     

Conclusion:

BNS-38 specifies that in extreme cases, such as when a person faces threats of death, grievous hurt, or certain serious crimes (rape, abduction, confinement, or acid attacks), they have the right to defend themselves using force, even if it results in the death of the attacker. This protection is subject to the limitations laid out in BNS-37 regarding proportionality and legality.
